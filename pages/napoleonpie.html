Where classic French pastry meets modern Japanese sweets. Despite its name, Napoleon Pie is often mistaken for a Russian dessert, but it was actually created in France to celebrate the victory over Emperor Napoleon. The original version contains no fruit—just crisp, flaky pastry layers paired with rich pastry cream. However, when the cake made its way to Japan, it was reimagined with seasonal fruits tucked between the layers of cream. With Napoleon desserts currently trending in Vietnam, why not try creating a more playful, modern twist of your own?

Follow The Directions

01

Step

Thaw one pastry sheet for about 10 minutes directly on a silicone mat placed on a baking tray. Dock the pastry thoroughly with a fork to allow steam to escape. Bake at 170°C for 10 minutes. Remove from oven, place a sheet of parchment paper on top, then set a heavy baking tray on top and press lightly. Return to oven and bake at 170°C for another 10 minutes. Remove the top tray and parchment, then bake again at 170°C for about 5 minutes, or until both top and bottom are evenly baked. Dust the surface with icing sugar and bake at maximum oven temperature (230°C or at least 200°C) for about 5 minutes, until the surface is caramelized. Let cool completely. The pastry may warp slightly while cooling. Trim edges to square the sheet, then cut in half. Repeat with the second pastry sheet. Crush pastry scraps into small flakes for decoration.

02

Step

Whisk egg yolks and sugar until pale and fluffy. Mix in cornstarch. Gradually pour the hot milk into the egg mixture while whisking. Return mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it boils and thickens. Remove from heat and mix in butter. Strain through a fine sieve into a bowl. Press plastic wrap directly onto the surface and refrigerate for 3–4 hours. Remove from fridge and whisk gently until smooth. Fold in whipped cream (80% whipped) using a whisk. Transfer to a piping bag.

03

Step

Slice strawberries lengthwise. Arrange two strawberry halves, cut-side down, aligned lengthwise on the first pastry sheet. Pipe custard cream into the gap between the two strawberries. Insert another strawberry half upright (cut-side up) between them. Pipe cream around and on top, filling low areas to create a flat surface. Smooth the surface gently with a palette knife. Place the next pastry sheet on top, aligning carefully, and smooth the sides. Repeat the filling process for the next layer. Place the final pastry sheet on top and smooth once more. Press pastry crumbs onto the sides of the cake. Pipe a thin line of cream to secure strawberries on top. Brush strawberries lightly with syrup or glaze for shine. Decorate the sides with extra whipped cream if desired.
