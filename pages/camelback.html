The best latte I’ve ever had was in Japan

If you’ve known me through Instagram, you might find it more familiar to hear me talk about coffee rather than baking. I originally started out creating content about coffee, not pastries. Over time, however, I lost my excitement for crafting elaborate drinks and began focusing instead on simple coffee—made with high-quality beans. My daily cups usually revolve around americanos and espressos, with an occasional latte. Because of that, I came to believe that it would be difficult for any cup of coffee to truly impress me, until I spent a year studying in Tokyo.

Oh my god, this is probably the best latte I've ever had!

That was the very first thing I blurted out after taking a sip of a caramel latte at Camelback in Tokyo. The rich, creamy milk blended beautifully with a salted caramel sauce—deeply bitter yet delicately sweet—paired with a fragrant shot of espresso. I was genuinely taken by surprise. And just look at the latte art on the surface. What a sight. The milk foam was so smooth it resembled a piece of lacquer art, a true testament to the barista’s exceptional craftsmanship. I drank that hot latte in the middle of summer without feeling overwhelmed by the heat at all—perhaps because it warmed my heart more than my body.

Other lattes that I really recommend:

I tried recreating a homemade version of that latte. While it may not be quite on the same level, it was more than enough to satisfy my taste buds.

  • 01.Make a salted caramel sauce using any recipe you like, but let the caramel cook a little longer than usual.
  • 02.Pull two shots of espresso and pour them over 25g of caramel sauce, then stir well.
  • 03.Add 150–200 ml of milk, hot or cold. Add ice if you’re making it iced.

If you ever find yourself in the Shinjuku area of Tokyo, make sure to stop by Swan. It’s a very small coffee shop, and you might have to wait up to an hour to get in, but their latte is absolutely worth it. I can’t quite remember whether they offer a caramel latte, but if Camelback’s caramel latte is its signature, then I believe Swan’s mocha is a worthy rival.
