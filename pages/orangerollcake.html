Orange Earl Grey Roll Cake

  • by Piceus
    • 9 / 10

  • COOK TIME
    1 Hour 30 Mins
  • SERVING

    3,450

Japanese-style roll cake with earl grey cream and whole mandarin inside. If you love cakes but find cream too rich, and crave the fresh, vibrant taste of fruit, this is the cake for you.

Ingredients

Roll Cake

Mandarin Oranges

Earl Grey Cream

Follow The Directions

01

Step

Separate the egg whites and yolks directly from the refrigerator, just before starting. Whip the egg whites at medium speed (3/5) throughout: Whip 30 seconds until foamy, then add ~10 g sugar. Whip 1 minute, add 15 g sugar. Then whip 1 minute and add the remaining sugar. Continue whipping for about 4 minutes until stable. Add the 3 egg yolks and mix at low speed for 1 minute. Sift in the cake flour and gently fold until incorporated (about 32 folds). Melt the butter and mix with the milk, take a small portion of the batter and mix it into the butter–milk mixture. Pour this mixture back into the main batter over a spatula to distribute evenly and avoid deflating. Fold gently until fully combined (about 36 folds). Pour the batter onto the prepared 28 cm baking sheet. Use a scraper to spread evenly. Tap the tray a few times to release large air bubbles. Bake at 160°C for 17 minutes.

02

Step

Heat the heavy cream until hot (do not boil). Add the Earl Grey tea leaves, cover, and infuse overnight in the refrigerator. Strain out the tea leaves, add sugar, and whip to stiff peaks. Place an ice pack or ice tray under the bowl to keep the cream cold while whipping.

03

Step

Place the cake sheet on parchment paper extending at least 15 cm longer than the cake. Peel off the brown surface layer of the sponge. Spread 150 g cream evenly over the cake, removing cream from the last 2 cm at the far edge. Arrange the 6 mandarin oranges in a single row slightly below the center, closer to the starting edge of the roll.

04

Step

Lift the parchment paper to raise the cake and roll tightly around the mandarins. Press gently with your hands to secure the fruit, then continue rolling. Use the reserved cream to fill and smooth both ends of the roll. Tighten the roll using a ruler or scraper. Place the seam side down, tape the parchment paper to secure, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Decorate with the remaining cream before serving.
