Orange Entremet

  • by Piceus

Inspired by the fruit-shaped cake trend in Los Angeles and Paris, I reinterpreted it with tropical Vietnamese fruits, such as this sweet mandarin-shaped cake. If you love my mango entremet recipe , make sure you don't skip these oranges as well!

Ingredients

Orange Marmalade

Chocolate Coating

Orange Ganache

Neutral Gel

Follow The Directions

01

Step

Mix the sugar with the agar-agar thoroughly. Combine the orange juice and water in a saucepan and heat until warm. While stirring constantly, slowly add the sugar–agar mixture, then bring to a full boil, then immediately pour into a bowl. Cover with cling film directly touching the surface and refrigerate until fully set. Cut the gel into small pieces using a spoon or spatula, then blend with a hand blender until completely smooth. Peel the oranges, remove all bitter white pith, and finely chop the peel. Dice the orange segments into small cubes. Add the chopped peel and diced pulp to the blended orange gel. Mix gently with a spatula, then briefly blend with a hand blender until combined but not completely smooth. Spoon the marmalade into six 4.6 cm half-sphere molds and freeze for 1 hour. Unmold the frozen halves, fill the molds again, place the frozen inserts on top, and freeze for 1–1.5 hours until fully set.

02

Step

Bloom the gelatin in cold water until fully hydrated. Heat the cream until just below boiling. Pour the hot cream over the white chocolate, add the bloomed gelatin, and blend with a hand blender until smooth. Add the orange juice and blend again until fully emulsified. Cover with cling film directly on the surface and refrigerate for 12 hours or overnight. Whip the ganache lightly to soft peaks (fluid, not holding stiff peaks). Pipe ganache into six 6 cm half-sphere molds, filling halfway. Insert the frozen orange marmalade centers. Freeze for 2–3 hours.

03

Step

Unmold the halves, pipe ganache halfway again, and invert the frozen halves on top. Freeze for 5 hours or overnight until fully solid. Unmold the cakes. Wearing gloves, trim excess edges with a knife and smooth the surface gently with gloved fingers, working one cake at a time while the others remain frozen. Angle the knife to carve a small hole on the top of each cake, then score five petal-like cuts as shown in the reference image. Freeze for several more hours until completely firm.

04

Step

Mix the sugar and pectin NH together. Heat the water with glucose syrup until dissolved, then remove from heat. While stirring, gradually add the sugar–pectin mixture. Bring to a boil and cook for 1 minute. Remove from heat, add citric acid, and mix well. Transfer to a bowl, cover directly with cling film, and refrigerate until cooled. Reheat in the microwave before spraying.

05

Step

Melt the white chocolate and cocoa butter together. Add orange and yellow fat-soluble food coloring. Dip the frozen cakes into the coating at 37–38°C (do not exceed 40°C). Rotate gently, remove the skewer immediately, and place on a silicone mat. Transfer cakes to the refrigerator while preparing the spray setup. Prepare a spray booth (foam box). Place a turntable lined with a silicone mat inside. Reheat remaining chocolate cocoa butter mixture to 45–50°C and spray evenly while rotating the cake. Switch spray gun and spray the neutral gel at 40°C.

06

Step

Dab a small amount of chocolate–cocoa butter mixture onto the base and attach the sprayed cake. Heat the metal tip of a stirring rod, insert it into the skewer hole, then attach the orange stem that has been dipped in chocolate coating to secure it in place.
