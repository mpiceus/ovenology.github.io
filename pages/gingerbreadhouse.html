Gingerbread House

Inspired by classic Victorian architecture, the recipe comes with a precisely measured template (PDF), a foolproof gingerbread cookie dough, and reliable royal icing for perfect assembly. With clear, step-by-step decorating instructions, this gingerbread house will have everyone around you in awe this Christmas season.

Follow The Directions

01

Step

Soften the butter, then mix with brown sugar on low speed using a hand mixer. (Alternatively, whisk by hand to avoid incorporating excess air). Add the molasses and mix until fully combined. In a separate bowl, mix together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and all the spices. Add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture in two additions, mixing with a spatula. Finish by pressing the dough together gently by hand until a cohesive dough forms. Wrap tightly and refrigerate for at least 3 hours or overnight.

02

Step

Divide the dough into portions. Lightly dust the silicone mat and dough with flour. Roll out to 3 mm thickness. Cut immediately, or freeze for 10–15 minutes before cutting for sharper edges. Preheat the oven to 175°C. Bake each piece individually for 4–9 minutes, adjusting time per piece. Bake until the edges are slightly darkened to ensure a dry, crisp cookie. Immediately after baking, trim the edges with a grater or sharp tool while still hot. Store cookies airtight with a desiccant packet until ready for icing.

03

Step

Bring the egg white to room temperature. Sift the powdered sugar in a separated bowl. Whip the egg white on the lowest speed for about 30 seconds until foamy. Gradually add powdered sugar, one spoon at a time, mixing on low speed. Continue until the icing flows slowly when lifting the whisk. Transfer half of the icing to a piping bag. Cover the remaining icing with a damp cloth to prevent drying. Consistency adjustment: Too thin → add powdered sugar, too thick → add a few drops of water or lemon juice.

04

Step

Decorate all gingerbread pieces with drawing-consistency icing. Let dry completely for at least 1 hour. Pipe stiff icing onto the base and attach the front wall. Support until half set. Attach side walls one by one, holding each joint 15–20 minutes until stable. Attach the back wall and let the structure dry 30–60 minutes before adding the roof.

05

Step

Attach roof panels one at a time using stiff icing, holding each for 30–45 minutes until fully set. Assemble the chimney separately, then attach it to the roof. Use drawing-consistency icing to pipe melting snow along the roof ridge and edges. Touch up decorations if needed. Dust flour over the roof to create a snow effect.
Light option: Cut a hole in the base before assembly. Insert the light after assembly and keep the switch outside.
