Banana Entremet

Mini entremet that looks exactly like banana, with chocolate cream and caramel sauce. It may take a bit of time to complete because it involves several small steps, but it’s actually quite easy—just a little patience is needed. I created a unique combination using brûléed white chocolate cream to add a subtle caramel note, and most people who try the cake find it unexpectedly delicious, without being able to guess how it was made.

Ingredients

Caramelized Banana Ganache

Chocolate Sponge

Banana Compote

Chocolate Coating

Follow The Directions

01

Step

Preheat the oven to 120°C (top and bottom heat). Line a baking tray with a silicone mat. Spread the white chocolate thinly on the mat, making sure it does not overflow the edges then bake for 10 minutes. Remove from the oven and mix with a spatula until smooth. Spread thinly again and bake for another 10 minutes. Repeat this process 2–3 more times, depending on the desired level of caramelization. Blend the caramelized white chocolate until completely smooth using a hand blender.

02

Step

Roast 100g of bananas at 175°C for 10 minutes until soft. Heat 60 g cream until hot and pour it over the warm caramelized white chocolate. Then, add the hot roasted banana and blend with a hand blender until smooth. Bloom the gelatin in cold water, then melt and add to the mixture. Allow the mixture to cool to below 35°C. Lightly whip the 140 g cold cream, then gently fold it into the ganache.

03

Step

Roast 125g of bananas at 175°C for 10 minutes. Mash with a fork; the texture does not need to be completely smooth. Bloom the gelatin in water, then stir it into the warm banana mixture until fully dissolved.

04

Step

Pipe the ganache to fill ⅔ of the banana mold. Use an offset spatula to spread the ganache up the sides, creating a cavity in the center, then it with banana compote. Pipe more ganache until nearly full, then place the chocolate sponge base on top. Smooth the surface and freeze until firm.

05

Step

Temper the white chocolate and melt the cocoa butter separately then combine them together in a jug. Add a few drops of yellow food coloring and blend until smooth. Let the coating cool to 35°C before use.

06

Step

Once the cake is deeply frozen, insert sticks into the banana shapes, quickly dip into the coating, then remove. Use a small brush and cocoa powder to create the banana details.
