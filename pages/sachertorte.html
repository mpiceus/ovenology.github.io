Orange Sacher Torte

  • by Piceus
    • 9 / 10

  • PREP TIME
    30 Mins
  • COOK TIME
    1 Hour 30 Mins
  • SERVING
    16cm (6 slices)
  • VIEWS
    3,450

This Austrian dessert is one of the best chocolate cakes in the world, even better with orange marmalade. The original version of this cake uses apricot jam, but I personally find it a bit too sweet, so I replaced it with orange marmalade and oh my, what a brilliant twist that turned out to be. I feel this cake especially suits middle-aged men. I once baked it for my father and also gave it to my teachers in Japan as a farewell gift before returning home, and one of them even teared up, saying it was the best chocolate cake they had ever had. So do give it a try as a birthday cake for your dad, or as a thoughtful gift to show appreciation to a teacher (or your boss, haha).

Ingredients

Cake Base

Orange Jam Layer & Syrup

Chocolate Glaze

Follow The Directions

01

Step

Line the base of a springform pan with baking paper. Preheat the oven to 180°C. Melt the dark chocolate and allow it to cool to about 35°C. Place the egg yolks in a small bowl and bring to room temperature. Store the egg whites in the freezer until ready to use.

02

Step

Soften the butter (surface temperature 20–22°C, not cold). Beat on medium speed until creamy. Add the confectioners’ sugar and mix on medium speed for 1 minute. Gradually add the egg yolks, mixing well after each addition. Pour in the lukewarm melted chocolate and mix on medium speed until fully incorporated. Whip the egg whites on medium speed until foamy and slightly thickened. Gradually add the sugar in three additions. Finish whipping on low speed for 30 seconds to eliminate large air bubbles. Add two spatulas of meringue into the chocolate batter. Sift in the cake flour and cocoa powder, then gently fold until combined. Add the remaining meringue and fold carefully until smooth.

03

Step

Pour the batter into the prepared pan and smooth the surface with a spatula. Bake at 160°C for 45 minutes in a preheated oven. Remove from the oven and loosen the sides of the pan. Carefully invert the cake onto a rack lined with baking paper and let cool for 20 minutes. Remove the baking paper, turn the cake upright, and allow to cool completely. Slice the cake horizontally into two layers, each about 2 cm thick.

04

Step

Take 120g orange jam and blend with 10 g water until smooth. Bring to a boil and cook until slightly thickened. Mix 10 g of the hot jam mixture with 10 g hot water to create a syrup. Brush the syrup evenly onto one cake layer. Spread 40g orange jam over the soaked cake layer. Place the second cake layer on top and brush with the remaining syrup. Pour the warm jam purée over the top of the cake and spread evenly over the surface and sides. (Place the cake on a cake ring base or stand it on a tall glass; line underneath with plastic wrap or parchment paper to catch excess jam.) Refrigerate for 1–2 hours until the jam layer is completely set and dry.

05

Step

Bring the sugar and water to a boil over medium-high heat and cook until 105–108°C. Remove from heat and stir gently with a spatula to cool slightly. Gradually add the dark chocolate and stir until thick and smooth. Allow the glaze to cool to 60–70°C. Pour the chocolate glaze over the cake in one motion, spreading as little as possible. Allow excess glaze to drip off. Trim the bottom edge once dripping stops. Let the glaze set completely. Use a large knife or scraper to carefully transfer the cake onto a serving board.

06

Step

[Optional decoration] Freeze metal stamps for at least 30 minutes before use. Prepare a flat dish lined with baking paper and freeze. Melt about 15 g dark chocolate (optional). Spoon 1 teaspoon or less onto the frozen surface, press the stamp gently, wait 20 seconds, then lift off. Freeze the chocolate stamp immediately for 1 hour. Attach the chocolate stamp onto the chilled cake using a small amount of orange jam.
