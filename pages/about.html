Mylopharyngodon Piceus

I am a self-taught baker who approaches baking as a form of scientific research, freely creating and experimenting within the rules of physics and chemistry.
I have spent the past four years teaching myself how to bake, learning through trial, failure, curiosity, and persistence. In recent years, short-term baking courses, long-term classes, and even professional pastry schools have become increasingly popular. While these paths can be valuable, I personally believe that anyone can create incredible baked goods on their own, if they are willing to invest time, patience, and genuine love for baking.
To me, baking is not just following recipes. It is about understanding why a cake rises, how textures are formed, and what happens when ingredients interact. With a solid grasp of the underlying science, creating new recipes is not only possible - it becomes exciting and reliable. Creativity does not come from guessing; it comes from knowledge.
I have dedicated most of my free time to studying baking books and resources in both English and Japanese, exploring the scientific principles behind each type of pastry. This research-driven approach allows me to minimize failure, adapt formulas confidently, and ensure that every recipe works as intended—not by luck, but by logic.
If you love baking and want to truly understand it, this space is for you. And if you don’t have much time but still want to create impressive, well-tested baked goods, this website is also made for you. Every recipe here is built on careful experimentation and scientific understanding, so you can bake with confidence.
