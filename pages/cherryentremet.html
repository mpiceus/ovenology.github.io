Cherry Entremet

Cherry-shaped mini entremet, with chocolate mousse cream, cherry jelly and mirror glaze on the outside.

Warning: this dessert is very complex to make! But if you want to impress someone, it’s definitely worth the challenge.

Ingredients

Cherry Jelly

Vanilla Sponge

Chocolate Coating

Chocolate Ganache Cream

Mirror Glaze

Chocolate Stem

Follow The Directions

01

Step

Heat cherries, sugar, and lemon juice until lightly boiling for a few minutes. Blend until smooth. Add the bloomed gelatin and stir until fully dissolved. Transfer to a piping bag or measuring cup and fill 3cm silicone molds. Freeze overnight or for at least 6 hours.

02

Step

Chop the dark chocolate finely. Pour in the hot milk, let sit for 1 minute, then whisk until smooth. (If not fully melted, microwave briefly until smooth.) Add the bloomed gelatin and mix well. Stir in the cold cream, mixing gently to combine. Cover with plastic wrap (cling film touching the surface) and refrigerate for 4–6 hours or overnight.

03

Step

[Vanilla Sponge] Whisk egg yolk with 6g sugar until pale and thick. Whip egg white with 16g sugar, added in 3 parts. Beat at medium-low for 30 sec each time, then 1 min on high until stiff peaks. Finish with low speed to stabilize the meringue. Gently fold the yolk mixture into the meringue without deflating, then sift in flour and fold until just combined. Add vanilla and oil, fold again carefully. Bake at 190°C for 8–10 minutes or until lightly golden and spongy. Let cool, then cut into small rounds slightly smaller than the mold (less than 3cm).

04

Step

Whip the ganache mousse until smooth and creamy then pipe mousse to fill ½–⅔ of the mold. Place one frozen cherry jelly insert in the center. Pipe more mousse almost to the top, then place the sponge base on top. Press gently to secure the shape, smooth the bottom with an offset spatula. Freeze for at least 6 hours or overnight. Unmold a few hours before glazing. Insert a skewer (rounded end preferred) into each cake and refreeze until firm.

05

Step

For chocolate coating, microwave chocolate and cocoa butter at 600W for 1 minute, stir, then continue heating until fully combined, cool to 40–45°C before use. For mirror glaze, heat sugar, water, and corn syrup over medium heat to 103°C then remove from heat and add white chocolate and condensed milk, let sit for 5 minutes. Microwave the bloomed gelatin for 15 sec at 800W, then add to the mixture. Add food coloring and stir gently to avoid air bubbles. Cover with cling film touching the surface and refrigerate at least 12 hours. For chocolate stem, temper the chocolate then pipe cherry stem shapes on parchment paper (pipe one line, then trace back to make it thicker and stronger). Attach small mint leaves at the end if desired.

06

Step

Prepare chocolate coating (40–45°C) and mirror glaze (30–32°C), working one cake at a time (keep others frozen). Dip pne into chocolate coating, spin slightly to drain excess, flatten the bottom on parchment. Immediately dip into mirror glaze and lift out. Pop any air bubbles immediately with a skewer for a smooth finish. Attach the chocolate stem and leaf (if not already attached).
