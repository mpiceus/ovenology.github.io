An amazing kissaten at the heart of Tokyo

Before coming to Japan, I had already encountered the word kissaten in my junior-high Japanese textbooks, where it was simply translated as “a refreshment shop.” At the time, the term didn’t carry any particular meaning for me. That perception completely changed once I set foot in Japan— specifically Tokyo, which can be considered the capital of kissaten culture. A kissaten is far more than a place that serves a few drinks or cakes; it is a miniature historical space, a snapshot of Japan in its golden era of the last century, where time seems to stand still amid a vivid fusion of Eastern and Western cultures.

Well, Kissa Hangetsu isn’t a traditional, old-school kissaten—but rather a creation by Japan’s younger generation, an attempt to revive and reinterpret the culture of the last century. Kissaten culture is honestly quite difficult to explain to Vietnamese readers, so for now, let’s focus on the cakes and drinks. Traditionally, kissaten did not have espresso machines, but Kissa Hangetsu does (after all, it’s a newly opened spot). I won’t go into specialty coffee beans here, as that isn’t really the spirit of a kissaten. What truly stands out instead is their creative use of alcohol in drinks. They offer a Cointreau latte—an orange liqueur latte—as well as whiskey latte and Cointreau-shaken Americano. I’ve visited this place many times simply because I absolutely love Cointreau.

One thing you simply can’t leave out when talking about kissaten is dessert. Most kissaten have their own signature sweets, and at Kissa Hangetsu, that signature is choux cream—but not just any ordinary one. Beneath the choux is a generous layer of fruit-filled cream made with seasonal fruits (strawberries, cherries, Shine Muscat grapes, and more), topped with a rich, beautifully piped swirl of cream. The one in the photo features a cherry and pistachio combination. The choux pastry itself is baked longer, giving it an extra-crispy shell. Honestly, this is one of the best choux creams I’ve ever had.

Do stop by if you ever find yourself in Tokyo. Kissa Hangetsu is tucked away in the Kuramae area of Taito Ward, just a short walk from the bustling tourist spots of Asakusa, yet surprisingly quiet and peaceful. That said, during peak hours you should still expect a fairly long wait. Alright then, I’m off to research how to recreate that incredible choux pastry now :) Be sure to check the Recipes section regularly—who knows, one day you might just find a cherry pistachio cream choux recipe there, haha.
