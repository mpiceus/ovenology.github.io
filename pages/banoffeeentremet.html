    Banoffee Entremet

    • November 1, 2025
    • by Piceus
      • 9 / 10
    • 02 Likes
    • PREP TIME
      30 Mins
    • COOK TIME
      3 Hours 30 Mins
    • SERVING
      5.8x3.5cm x8
    • VIEWS
      3,450

    Impressive mini entremet with a deep flavor of dark chocolate, salted caramel and ripped banana. I’m quite proud of this cake, as it is one of the few creations I developed entirely from scratch, without referencing any existing desserts. After traveling to many countries, I came to realize how exceptional Vietnamese bananas truly are. They deserve more creative desserts that celebrate this seemingly ordinary yet deeply familiar and special fruit.

    Ingredients

    Banoffee Compote

    Chocolate Sponge

    Chocolate Light Mousse

    Chocolate Mirror Glaze

    Chocolate Cream

    Garnish

    Follow The Directions

    01

    Step

    Roast 100 g banana at 170°C for 10 minutes. While both are still hot, blend the roasted banana into the warm caramel sauce until smooth. Dice the remaining 140 g banana into small cubes and fold into the banana caramel mixture. Spoon into 8 hemisphere molds (4.6 cm). The recipe yields slightly more than needed. Freeze until fully set.

    02

    Step

    Bloom gelatin in cold water and refrigerate until fully hydrated. Heat milk to about 80°C, then pour over finely chopped dark chocolate. Let stand 1 minute, then stir until smooth to form a ganache. Add bloomed gelatin (melt briefly in the microwave if needed) and mix well. Let the mixture cool to 32–34°C. Whip cold cream to soft peaks (yogurt-like, softly flowing texture). Gently fold the whipped cream into the chocolate mixture. Fill molds halfway with mousse, insert frozen banoffee compote, then fill with more mousse if needed. Place a disc of chocolate sponge on top and press gently to fully fill the mold. Freeze for at least 8 hours or overnight.

    03

    Step

    Bloom gelatin in cold water. In a saucepan, combine sugar and sifted cocoa powder. Add water and cook over medium heat, whisking until smooth. Switch to a spatula and scrape the bottom continuously to prevent scorching. Once simmering, gradually add cream while stirring. Bring back to a boil. Remove from heat once boiling vigorously, strain through a fine sieve into a bowl. Add bloomed gelatin and stir until fully dissolved. Cool glaze to 40°C before glazing.

    04

    Step

    Temper dark chocolate to 45°C → 29°C → 33°C. Prepare a firm acetate sheet. Dip the tip of a small knife into tempered chocolate, scrape off chocolate from one side. Press the knife onto the acetate, lift slightly while chocolate adheres, then pull straight down to form a strip. Slightly curve the acetate sheet and allow chocolate to set. Use a warm knife tip to carve feather-like details if desired. Slice banana into round pieces. Sprinkle with sugar and torch until caramelized.

    05

    Step

    Heat 50 g cream and pour over dark chocolate, stirring until smooth. Add remaining cream and mix well. Chill completely, then whip until light and pipeable.

    06

    Step

    Unmold frozen cakes and keep in the freezer. Remove only right before glazing. Dip cakes into mirror glaze or pour glaze over them depending on glaze consistency. Slide an offset spatula under the base, gently rotate on a rack to remove excess glaze, then transfer to serving bases. Pipe chocolate cream on top using a Saint-Honoré or twisted nozzle. Place a brûléed banana slice in the center and arrange chocolate garnishes around.
