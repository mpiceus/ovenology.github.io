The ultimate apple pie of a 100-year old kissaten

I’ve already introduced kissaten culture in my previous blog post about Kissa Hangetsu, haven’t I? Unlike Hangetsu, this place is a true kissaten, with a history spanning more than 120 years. Located in the heart of upscale Ginza, Tricolore embodies the authentic spirit of kissaten at the height of its golden era, dating back to Japan’s World War period. Setting politics and war aside, let’s talk about the café’s legendary apple pie. I returned to this café five times within a single year, and every single visit, I had to order this pie—once I even had two because it was just that good. The filling is generously packed with apples, baked until tender yet still pleasantly crisp, with a perfectly balanced sweet-and-tart flavor. The puff pastry rises beautifully, enveloping the overflowing filling, making it an absolute feast for the eyes as well as the palate.

Tricolore has held the title of a top-rated establishment on Tabelog for many years (Japan’s largest food review platform). If you’d like to experience an afternoon tea reminiscent of Japan’s imperial-era elite, be sure to stop by on your next trip. Don’t be discouraged by a long line—trust me, many people give up along the way, and you’ll likely only wait about 20–30 minutes (a regular’s firsthand experience).
