Orange Earl Grey Swan Cake

An elegant cake with the classic combination of flavors. This cake can be considered a combination of two pastries: gâteau and choux. Although it may take a bit of time, the steps themselves are quite straightforward, and the final result is guaranteed to be truly impressive.

Ingredients

Earl Grey Sponge

Earl Grey Cream

Swan Pâte à Choux

Mandarin Oranges

Follow The Directions

01

Step

Microwave milk until boiling. Add Earl Grey tea and infuse for 30 minutes. Strain and use 30 g of the infused milk. Mix with oil and warm before use. Sift cake flour and Earl Grey tea leaves together onto parchment paper. Preheat oven to 150°C. Line the cake pan. Whip egg whites on medium speed (3/5): 30 seconds until foamy, add ~10 g sugar, Whip 1 minute, add 15 g sugar, Whip 1 minute, add remaining sugar, Continue whipping ~4 minutes until stable, glossy meringue. Add egg yolks and mix on low speed for 1 minute. Sift in dry ingredients and fold gently (~32 folds). Mix 1 spoonful batter into the oil–milk mixture until smooth, then pour back and fold (~36 folds). Pour batter from 15 cm height into the pan, tap to release air. Bake at 150°C for 40 minutes. Immediately tap the pan again, cover cake with parchment, invert onto a rack and cover with a damp towel. Once completely cool, wrap tightly and chill 2–3 hours. Slice into 3 layers (1–1.5 cm thick).

02

Step

Combine cream, tea, and sugar. Whip on low speed (speed 2) until very soft peaks. Keep cream chilled over an ice tray until assembly. Peel mandarins. Separate segments and remove excess membrane if needed.

03

Step

[Swan Choux] Bring water, milk, butter, sugar, and salt to a rolling boil.Remove from heat, add flour all at once, and mix thoroughly. Return to low heat and cook, stirring constantly, until a thin film forms on the bottom (~4 minutes). Transfer dough to a bowl and cool 10–15 minutes. Gradually add beaten eggs until the dough falls in a V-shape from the spatula. Draw S-shapes (necks) and teardrops (bodies) on parchment. Dust bodies lightly with powdered sugar before baking. Bake: Bodies at 190°C for 25 minutes and Necks at 170°C for 5 minutes or until lightly golden. Cool completely. Slice bodies horizontally, then cut the top portion in half to form wings.

04

Step

Spread a thin layer of Earl Grey cream on the first sponge layer. Arrange mandarin segments in a ring, then cover completely with cream. Repeat with the second layer. Place the final sponge layer on top. Frost the entire cake (top and sides). Pipe decorative cream using a Saint-Honoré tip around the cake. Place one swan choux (piped cream inside as well) in the center as the final garnish.
